# Finance Literature Review Context

When a task involves finance, macro-finance, banking, accounting, corporate finance, asset pricing, disclosure, governance, or adjacent economics literature, use a finance-first review workflow.

## Default Search Order

1. Top published finance journals.
2. Adjacent top economics journals if the mechanism or identification strategy overlaps.
3. Top accounting journals if the topic overlaps disclosure, earnings, auditing, governance, or information environment.
4. Working-paper series such as NBER, CEPR, SSRN, and RePEc / IDEAS for frontier coverage.
5. Broad metadata indices only after the venue ladder is defined.

## Hard Rules

- Never invent citations.
- Separate published papers from working papers.
- Track identification strategy, sample, period, unit of observation, main result, mechanism, and limitation.
- Prefer synthesis by question, design, and mechanism over paper-by-paper summaries.
- If the evidence base is thin or frontier-heavy, say that directly.

## Deliverables

- scoped research question,
- search plan,
- prioritized paper list,
- literature matrix,
- synthesis and gap statement.
